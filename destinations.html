Destinations

Our Philosophy

The Last Shangri-La

Bhutan measures its success not in GDP but in Gross National Happiness. Every destination here carries that philosophy — unhurried, authentic, and deeply connected to nature and tradition. Our local guides know each valley, trail, and dzong intimately.

Iconic Western Bhutan

Paro Valley

Home of the iconic Tiger's Nest

Paro is Bhutan's most visited valley and for good reason. The Paro Taktsang monastery — Tiger's Nest — clings to a sheer cliff 900 metres above the valley floor. Beyond the monastery, Paro offers rice paddies, ancient dzongs, and the country's only international airport.

Highlights

  • Tiger's Nest Monastery
  • Paro Dzong
  • National Museum of Bhutan
  • Drukgyel Dzong ruins
March – May, Sep – Nov Moderate
Capital Western Bhutan

Thimphu

The capital with no traffic lights

Thimphu is the world's only capital city without a single traffic light — police officers direct traffic at the main intersection. It blends ancient Bhutanese tradition with a quietly modern sensibility. Explore weekend markets, the giant Buddha Dordenma statue, and the vibrant arts scene.

Highlights

  • Buddha Dordenma Statue
  • Tashichho Dzong
  • Weekend Market
  • National Textile Museum
Year-round Easy
Heritage Central Bhutan

Punakha

The ancient capital of Bhutan

Punakha served as Bhutan's capital until 1955 and remains one of the most breathtaking destinations in the country. The Punakha Dzong, built at the confluence of two rivers, is considered the most beautiful dzong in Bhutan. The valley is warmer and lower than most, making it ideal year-round.

Highlights

  • Punakha Dzong
  • Chimi Lhakhang
  • Suspension Bridge
  • Khamsum Yulley Namgyal Chorten
Feb – Mar, Oct – Dec Easy
Spiritual Central Bhutan

Bumthang Valley

The spiritual heartland of Bhutan

Bumthang is considered the spiritual heartland of Bhutan, home to some of the country's oldest and most sacred temples. The valley's four sub-valleys — Chokhor, Tang, Ura, and Chhume — each have their own distinct character, from buckwheat fields to ancient lhakhangs.

Highlights

  • Jambay Lhakhang
  • Kurjey Lhakhang
  • Tamshing Monastery
  • Ura Village
Apr – Jun, Sep – Nov Easy to Moderate
Wildlife Central Bhutan

Gangtey & Phobjikha

Valley of the black-necked cranes

The Phobjikha valley is a glacial valley and one of the most important wildlife preserves in Bhutan. Every winter, endangered black-necked cranes migrate here from the Tibetan plateau. The valley is also home to the magnificent Gangtey Monastery, perched on a forested hill above the wetlands.

Highlights

  • Black-necked Crane Festival (Nov)
  • Gangtey Monastery
  • Crane Information Centre
  • Valley hiking trails
Oct – Feb (crane season) Easy
Off the Beaten Path Western Bhutan

Haa Valley

Bhutan's most remote and pristine valley

Only opened to tourists in 2002, Haa Valley remains one of Bhutan's least-visited and most pristine destinations. Bordered by high Himalayan peaks and dense forests, it offers exceptional trekking, traditional village life, and a rare glimpse of Bhutan untouched by tourism.

Highlights

  • Lhakhang Karpo (White Temple)
  • Lhakhang Nagpo (Black Temple)
  • Haa Summer Festival
  • High-altitude trekking
May – Sep Moderate to Challenging
