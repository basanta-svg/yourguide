Tour Package

Cultural Immersion Tour

7 Days 2–10 people

A complete 7-day circuit through western Bhutan — Paro, Thimphu, the glacial Phobjikha Valley, and Punakha — blending iconic dzongs, sacred monasteries, and the endangered black-necked cranes of the Himalaya's most tranquil valley, before a hike to the cliffside Tiger's Nest.

Itinerary

Day by Day

Upon your arrival at Paro International Airport, you will be warmly welcomed by your professional local Bhutanese tour guide before beginning a scenic drive to Thimphu, the capital of Bhutan. En route, stop at the historic Tachhog Lhakhang Iron Bridge, built in the 15th century by the legendary Tibetan saint Thangtong Gyalpo, who is famous for constructing iron chain bridges across the Himalayas to improve travel and pilgrimage routes. Continue your journey to Thimphu, where you will enjoy a delicious traditional Bhutanese lunch at a local farmhouse and experience authentic Bhutanese hospitality. In the afternoon, explore the vibrant Thimphu Main Market (Norzin Lam), where you can stroll through local shops and observe everyday Bhutanese life. In the evening, visit the magnificent Tashichho Dzong, originally built in 1641 by Zhabdrung Ngawang Namgyal, the unifier of Bhutan, and today serving as the seat of the King's office and the Central Monastic Body. Before exploring the fortress, witness the traditional Flag Lowering Ceremony, a beautiful daily military ritual held at the Dzong. Afterwards, transfer to your hotel for an overnight stay in Thimphu.

Overnight in Thimphu

After breakfast, begin your sightseeing at the National Memorial Chorten, built in 1974 in memory of Bhutan's Third King, His Majesty Jigme Dorji Wangchuck, who is widely regarded as the Father of Modern Bhutan. Continue to the iconic Buddha Dordenma (Buddha Point), a magnificent 51.5-metre bronze statue completed in 2015, overlooking the Thimphu Valley and housing over 100,000 smaller Buddha statues as a symbol of peace and prosperity. Next, visit the Motithang Takin Preserve Centre, home to Bhutan's unique national animal, the Takin, which according to legend was created by the Divine Madman, Drukpa Kunley. Afterwards, drive to Sangaygang Viewpoint, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the entire Thimphu Valley. Following lunch, visit the National Institute for Zorig Chusum, where students are trained in Bhutan's thirteen traditional arts and crafts, preserving centuries-old cultural traditions. Continue to the Bhutan Postal Museum, where you can learn about Bhutan's unique postal history and even create personalized postage stamps. End the day by exploring the lively Centenary Farmers' Market, where local farmers sell fresh produce, handicrafts, and regional specialties, before returning to your hotel.

Overnight in Thimphu

After breakfast, depart for the beautiful Phobjikha Valley, travelling through the scenic Dochula Pass (3,100 m). The pass is renowned for its 108 Druk Wangyal Chortens, built in 2004 by the Queen Mother, Ashi Dorji Wangmo Wangchuck, in memory of Bhutanese soldiers. On a clear day, the pass offers spectacular panoramic views of the snow-capped Himalayan mountain range. Continue your journey via Wangdue Phodrang before arriving in the peaceful Phobjikha Valley, one of Bhutan's most beautiful glacial valleys and the winter home of the endangered Black-necked Cranes. In the evening, enjoy a relaxing walk along the Gangtey Nature Trail, passing through pine forests, open meadows, and traditional villages while taking in the valley's tranquil scenery. Afterwards, check in to your hotel for an overnight stay.

Overnight in Phobjikha

After breakfast, visit the Black-necked Crane Information Centre, where you will learn about the endangered Black-necked Cranes that migrate from the Tibetan Plateau to Phobjikha Valley every winter, making it one of the most important bird conservation sites in the Himalayas. Continue to the historic Gangtey Monastery, founded in the early 17th century and one of the most significant Nyingma monasteries in Bhutan. Afterwards, drive to Punakha, the ancient capital of Bhutan. Upon arrival, visit the magnificent Punakha Dzong, built in 1637 by Zhabdrung Ngawang Namgyal, beautifully located at the confluence of the Pho Chhu and Mo Chhu rivers and regarded as one of Bhutan's finest architectural masterpieces. Later, enjoy a walk across the Punakha Suspension Bridge, one of the longest suspension bridges in Bhutan, offering stunning views of the river and surrounding valley before checking into your hotel.

Overnight in Punakha

After breakfast, visit the sacred Chimi Lhakhang (Fertility Temple), built in 1499 in honour of the famous Buddhist saint Drukpa Kunley, affectionately known as the Divine Madman, and widely believed to bless couples seeking children. Afterwards, drive back to Paro through the scenic mountain highways. Upon arrival, visit the National Museum of Bhutan (Ta Dzong), originally built as a watchtower in 1649 and now home to an impressive collection of Bhutanese art, cultural artifacts, and historical treasures. Continue to the majestic Rinpung Dzong, built in 1646 by Zhabdrung Ngawang Namgyal, one of Bhutan's most important fortresses and the venue for the famous Paro Tshechu Festival. In the evening, enjoy a leisurely stroll through Paro Town, where you can explore local handicraft shops and cafés before returning to your hotel.

Overnight in Paro

After an early breakfast, embark on the unforgettable hike to Taktsang Monastery (Tiger's Nest), Bhutan's most sacred and iconic monastery, dramatically perched on a cliff at an elevation of 3,120 metres, about 900 metres above the Paro Valley. According to legend, Guru Padmasambhava (Guru Rinpoche) flew to this site on the back of a tigress in the 8th century and meditated in a cave for three months, introducing Buddhism to Bhutan. The monastery, first built in 1692, has since become the country's most revered pilgrimage site. The round-trip hike takes approximately 5–6 hours, passing through beautiful pine forests, prayer flags, and spectacular viewpoints before rewarding visitors with breathtaking views of the monastery and surrounding valley. After the hike, return to Paro for an overnight stay.

Overnight in Paro

After breakfast, transfer to Paro International Airport for your onward flight. As your Bhutan journey comes to an end, depart with unforgettable memories of Bhutan's breathtaking landscapes, ancient monasteries, vibrant culture, warm hospitality, and the timeless spirit of the Land of the Thunder Dragon.
