Tour Package

Bhutan Cultural Journey

6 Days 2–10 people

A compact 6-day circuit through Paro, Thimphu, and Punakha (Paro – Thimphu – Punakha – Paro) — Bhutan's dzongs, sacred temples, and living traditions, capped with a hike to the cliffside Tiger's Nest Monastery.

Itinerary

Day by Day

Upon your arrival at Paro International Airport, you will be warmly welcomed by your professional local Bhutanese tour guide before beginning a scenic drive to Thimphu, the capital city of Bhutan. En route, stop at the historic Tachhog Lhakhang Iron Bridge, built in the 15th century by the legendary Tibetan saint Thangtong Gyalpo, renowned for constructing iron chain bridges across the Himalayas to connect remote communities and pilgrimage routes. Continue your journey to Thimphu and enjoy a delicious traditional Bhutanese lunch at a local restaurant or farmhouse. In the afternoon, explore the lively Norzin Lam (Thimphu Main Market), where you can experience the local lifestyle, browse handicraft shops, and interact with friendly locals. In the evening, visit the magnificent Tashichho Dzong, originally built in 1641 by Zhabdrung Ngawang Namgyal, the unifier of Bhutan, and now the seat of the King's Office and the Central Monastic Body. Before entering the fortress, witness the impressive Flag Lowering Ceremony, a daily military tradition held within the Dzong grounds. Afterwards, transfer to your hotel for an overnight stay in Thimphu.

Overnight in Thimphu

After breakfast, begin the day with a visit to the sacred National Memorial Chorten, built in 1974 in memory of Bhutan's Third King, His Majesty Jigme Dorji Wangchuck, who is remembered as the Father of Modern Bhutan. Continue to the iconic Buddha Dordenma (Buddha Point), a magnificent 51.5-metre bronze statue completed in 2015, overlooking the Thimphu Valley and housing more than 100,000 smaller Buddha statues as a symbol of peace and harmony. Next, visit the Motithang Takin Preserve Centre, home to Bhutan's national animal, the Takin, which according to local legend was created by the revered saint Drukpa Kunley. Afterwards, drive to Sangaygang Viewpoint, where you can enjoy spectacular panoramic views of the entire Thimphu Valley. Following lunch, visit the National Institute for Zorig Chusum, where students learn Bhutan's thirteen traditional arts and crafts that have been preserved for generations. Continue to the Bhutan Postal Museum, showcasing the country's unique postal history and famous collectible stamps. End the day with a visit to the vibrant Centenary Farmers' Market, where local farmers sell fresh produce, spices, cheeses, and traditional Bhutanese products before returning to your hotel.

Overnight in Thimphu

After breakfast, drive towards the beautiful valley of Punakha, crossing the scenic Dochula Pass (3,100 m). The pass is famous for its 108 Druk Wangyal Chortens, built in 2004 by Her Majesty the Queen Mother, Ashi Dorji Wangmo Wangchuck, in memory of Bhutanese soldiers. On a clear day, enjoy breathtaking views of the eastern Himalayan mountain range, including Gangkar Puensum (7,570 m), the highest mountain in Bhutan and the world's highest unclimbed peak. Continue to the sacred Chimi Lhakhang (Fertility Temple), built in 1499 in honour of the famous Buddhist saint Drukpa Kunley, affectionately known as the Divine Madman and revered for blessing couples wishing to have children. After lunch, visit the magnificent Punakha Dzong, built in 1637 by Zhabdrung Ngawang Namgyal, beautifully situated at the confluence of the Pho Chhu and Mo Chhu rivers and regarded as one of Bhutan's finest architectural masterpieces. Later, enjoy a leisurely walk across the Punakha Suspension Bridge, the longest suspension bridge in Bhutan, offering stunning views of the river and surrounding valley before checking in to your hotel.

Overnight in Punakha

After breakfast, drive back towards Paro through Bhutan's scenic mountain landscapes. En route, stop at Chuzom, the picturesque confluence where the Paro Chhu and Wang Chhu rivers meet. This important junction marks the crossroads connecting Paro, Thimphu, and Haa, and is distinguished by three beautiful chortens built in Bhutanese, Tibetan, and Nepalese architectural styles. Upon arrival in Paro, visit the National Museum of Bhutan (Ta Dzong), originally built as a watchtower in 1649 and now home to an extensive collection of Bhutanese art, history, cultural artefacts, and religious treasures. Continue to the magnificent Rinpung Dzong, built in 1646 by Zhabdrung Ngawang Namgyal, one of Bhutan's finest fortresses and the venue of the renowned Paro Tshechu Festival. In the evening, enjoy a leisurely stroll through Paro Town, where you can browse handicraft shops, cafés, and local boutiques before returning to your hotel.

Overnight in Paro

After an early breakfast, embark on Bhutan's most iconic excursion to Taktsang Monastery (Tiger's Nest), perched dramatically on a cliff at an elevation of 3,120 metres, approximately 900 metres above the Paro Valley. According to legend, Guru Padmasambhava (Guru Rinpoche) flew to this sacred site on the back of a tigress in the 8th century and meditated in a cave for three months, introducing Buddhism to Bhutan. The monastery, first built in 1692, is Bhutan's most sacred pilgrimage site and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valley. The round-trip hike takes approximately 5–6 hours, passing through fragrant pine forests, colourful prayer flags, and scenic viewpoints before returning to Paro for an overnight stay.

Overnight in Paro

After breakfast, transfer to Paro International Airport for your departure flight. As your memorable journey through the Kingdom of Bhutan comes to an end, depart with unforgettable memories.
